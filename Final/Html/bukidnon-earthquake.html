Several Bukidnon school buildings damaged by 7.4-magnitude earthquake

VALENCIA CITY, Bukidnon — Several government and private school buildings in this city sustained structural damage following the magnitude 7.4 earthquake that struck off the coast of Manay, Davao Oriental, at 9:43 a.m. on October 10.

In its consolidated report, the City Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (CDRRMO) said at least 13 schools and public facilities across 11 barangays were affected by the tremor.

A total of 26 classrooms, along with several offices, stages, and barangay halls, sustained cracks or partial structural damage.

The most affected areas were Mt. Nebo, Bagontaas and Kahaponan villages, which each reported multiple classrooms with visible cracks.

Other barangays with reported damages include Batangan, Sinabuagan, Concepcion, Laligan, Lumbayao, Sinalayan, Tugaya, and Nabag-o.

CDRRMO chief June Ray Valero said an infrastructure audit team composed of national and local engineers will be organized to assess the integrity of both private and government structures, particularly vital facilities and establishments.

Valero also emphasized the need to establish organized disaster response teams in every workplace and school with a sufficient number of personnel to strengthen preparedness for both natural and human-induced disasters.

He likewise called for the activation of the Community-Based Monitoring System to support local inspection and data validation efforts.
